[INSIDE MUSIC]


1.Every Friday, I highlight just a few of the most notable new songs and album releases:

The 1975 - "Notes On A Conditional Form"
Tove Lo - "Sadder Badder Cooler"
Neon Trees - "New Best Friend"

The 1975's latest album has been described by the band as an end of an era as it completes the previous two in message and style. More importantly, is it good? Across 13 reviews listed on Metacritic, the album has received a 69/100 rating — 7 positive, 4 neutral, and 2 negative. With 22 songs spanning 80 minutes, it has time to wander and be all over the place, but it's very on point for the band. If you've liked the past few albums, you should enjoy this one.

Additionally, The 1975 has made some stems from its new album available for people to remix songs as part of its weird Second Life-like site, Mindshower.ai. (Hint: look at the computer behind the desk.)

Alternative band Neon Trees has a surprise new song out today called "New Best Friend. " And Tove Lo re-released her last album with a new track called, "Sadder Badder Cooler."

2. Album announcements:
  • Disclosure announced the group's new album "Energy" will come out on August 28. They shared the first single, also named "Energy," along with an impressive list of guests including Slowthai, Common, Mick Jenkins, Aminé, Channel Tres, Kelis, Syd of The Internet, and Fatoumata Diawara, among others.

  • Lana Del Rey's new, as yet untitled album, will be released on September 5. Del Rey shared the news on Instagram, but it was overshadowed by her statement refuting claims that she glamorizes abuse. Her post mentions Doja Cat, Ariana Grande, Camila Cabello, Cardi B, Kehlani, Nicki Minaj, and Beyoncé by name, comparing these other female artists and their lyrical content. The post has sparked some debate with over 107,000 comments as of this writing.

  • If you missed it, Carly Rae Jepsen released a surprise new album “Dedicated Side B” yesterday. While b-side songs, or tracks that didn’t make it on to the record, will usually see the light of day in some capacity, it's now becoming common for Jepsen to put out a second album of her "b-sides." Not only did Jepsen release "Emotion Side B" in 2016, but the eight tracks were really well received by fans.
